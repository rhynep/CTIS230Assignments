A Spiritual War Forgotten: Nazi Religiosity through the Eyes of American Media

By Pamela Michelle Rhyne

Abstract: Although little historiography is available, the religiosity of Adolf Hitler and the Third Reich is a significant element of history. Nazi ideology infiltrated all dimensions of German life, until the church and state essentially began to blend as one. Through the lens of American press, the topic was of the upmost concern. An abundance of press regarding the spiritual dynamics of Nazism is available and the publications contain reactions from a diverse assortment of theologians, politicians, and academics. With a primary source base from the New York Times and The Christian Century, this thesis is an analysis of how American media grappled with transformations in religious policy and the religiosity of Nazism between the years 1933 and 1942, and thus offers a new chapter of United States history.
